How a Search Engine Gathers its Information

Based on each search engine’s unique algorithm, your site will be ranked and your SERPs (Search Engine Results Pages) results returned based upon keyword requests from users. Search engines send out little programmes known as 'robots' or 'bots' or 'spiders' to crawl the Internet in search of websites and their individual web pages.

These programmes gather all the information on your website that they can find, or that you allow them to see. Information gathered by search engine bots then gets cached (stored) in the search engine's servers, where the web pages are categorised in order of importance. Search engines classify the content on web pages by using reference points such as the page title, description, paragraph headers, and other usability information.

Search engines always strive to deliver the most relevant results to the search request the user has made. A search engine that consistently delivers good results gets visited more often, and gains trust in the eyes of the user. One of the ways that Google became so successful was that it found ways to index credible websites while eliminating search results that were less relevant. One of Google's methods for establishing user trust has been to count the quantity and quality of links to any given website from other pages on the Internet, and to use a formula for doing this automatically. This formula is known as the Google Algorithm. This algorithm is constantly changed and tweaked to provide the best user experience in terms of producing highly relevant results, and also to thwart unscrupulous use of 'black hat' search engine optimisation techniques to 'game' the system in an attempt to position web pages higher in the rankings than they would naturally occur.

Natural Search Results

The natural search results are those organic results that are generated by search engine searches and are listed directly below the sponsored ads. In addition to your actual website, other things can appear in the natural search results.For this reason, you need to pay attention to the use of keywords in the following because they are all commonly found ranked in natural search results: Articles, Off-site blogs, Press releases, Social networking profiles, Social bookmarking profiles and videos.

How Will People Search for You?

What types of phrasing will potential customers use when searching for you? Will they look for a person, position, a firm or a solution? Will they use geographic modifiers like city or county? Will their level of motivation come through in their search, by using specific words?

  • Specific person: “Plumber”, “plumbing contractor”, etc.
  • Geographic modifiers: “plumber”, “plumber Cork”
  • Motivation levels: “emergency plumber Cork”, “24 hour plumber”.

As a critical part of your keyword research, you should employ a keyword research. While there are a variety of such tools on the market, Google AdWords has a free one that can help greatly, as it generates keyword ideas for you, as well as synonyms tool.

Cross-reference your keywords with geographic modifiers. If your business is in Cork, as in the plumber example, people too far away most likely won’t call your business to engage your services. That’s where geographic modifiers come in. City names, county names and countries are all geographic modifiers. Your audience is likely to search for your business using these geographic modifiers or terms because they want to find somebody locally that can fulfill their needs. This means that in your keyword research process, you should also include geography as part of your criteria.

Choosing a Domain Name

Branding your business by means of your domain name is important. Choosing a domain name that is easy to recognise and easy to find means that your online presence will be much more dynamic. Great domains are ones that have your main service or product type and your location in them.

For example, if your main service is the provision of liposuction and your clinic is in Cork, the perfect web domain would be www.LiposuctionCork.ie, or a variation of this. You may have to do some digging to get a good domain name. Another alternative is to see if the name of your business is available as a domain name.

Meta Tags

The meta tags are a very important part of the code of your website. They are readby the search engines, but are not visible as part of your web page content as theyare in the code of the website. When your meta tags are properly configured thisgives the search engines a concise summary of the web page content so the searchengines understand what your website is about and rank it accordingly.Most meta tags are included within the 'header' html code of a website. The mostimportant tags are the title, description and keywords.

Backlinking

In search engine optimisation (SEO), backlinks refer to hyperlinks pointing to a particular web page. Backlinks are important to SEO because search engine considers the quality and quantity of backlinks to determine page rank. Here is how backlinking works, some of the different types of links there are and how they all pull together to move your key phrases and their associated web pages up through the search engine rankings.

Not all links are created equal. Google and the other search engines have a rating system whereby a link coming from a website with a high page rank is considered to be far more important than a link coming from a website or blog with a low page rank. For example, one link from a website with a page rank of 9 would be worth thousands of individual links coming from blogs with a page rank of 0.

YouTube is another site with a high authority ranking. Uploading well optimised videos with a link back to your website has always been considered a good strategy for high quality backlinks, especially since Google is the owner of YouTube!

Bookmark links are social links. These will generally give a lower quality of link back to your website, but are necessary to the linking mix. It would look far too unnatural to the search engines to only have links coming to your site and pages from sites with higher page authority. There are literally hundreds of bookmarking sites; some of the better known ones are Del.icio.us, StumbleUpon and Ma.gnolia.
